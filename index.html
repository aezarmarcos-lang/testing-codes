Definitions

Types and Definitions of STIs
Bacterial STI Definition Parasitic STI Definition
Chlamydia A very common STI that often shows no symptoms. It spreads through sexual intercourse and is treatable with antibiotics, but may cause infertility or PID. Trichomoniasis Caused by the parasite Trichomonas vaginalis. Symptoms include itching, strong-smelling discharge, and painful urination.
Gonorrhea A bacterial infection affecting the rectum, throat, or eyes. It may be asymptomatic and requires antibiotic treatment. Scabies A skin condition caused by mites that spread through skin-to-skin contact, causing intense itching.
Syphilis Caused by Treponema pallidum. It progresses in stages and can affect multiple organs. Known as the “Great Imitator”. Pubic Lice Small parasites that infest pubic hair, feed on blood, and cause intense itching.
HIV A virus that attacks the immune system and may lead to AIDS if untreated. It spreads through infected bodily fluids. Amebiasis & Giardiasis Intestinal infections caused by protozoan parasites, spread through oral-anal contact.
