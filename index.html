Definitions and Key Characteristics

Common STIs and Their Medical Definitions
STI Type Medical Definition
Chlamydia Bacterial A common bacterial infection often without symptoms. It spreads through sexual contact and is treatable with antibiotics, but untreated cases may lead to pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) and infertility.
Gonorrhea Bacterial An infection affecting the genitals, rectum, throat, or eyes. It may be asymptomatic and requires prompt antibiotic treatment to prevent complications.
Syphilis Bacterial Caused by Treponema pallidum. It progresses through distinct stages and can affect multiple organs, earning the nickname “the Great Imitator.”
HIV Viral A virus that attacks the immune system and may progress to AIDS if untreated. It is transmitted through bodily fluids and managed with lifelong antiretroviral therapy.
Trichomoniasis Parasitic Caused by Trichomonas vaginalis. Symptoms may include genital itching, irritation, abnormal discharge, and painful urination.
Scabies Parasitic A skin infestation caused by microscopic mites, spread through prolonged skin-to-skin contact, leading to intense itching and rash.
Pubic Lice Parasitic Small parasitic insects that infest pubic hair, feed on blood, and cause persistent itching.
Amebiasis & Giardiasis Parasitic Intestinal infections caused by protozoan parasites, transmitted through contaminated food, water, or oral-anal sexual contact.
